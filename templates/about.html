About this Website

What's this?

This website aggregates the official battlefield 3 forums and the twitter accounts of developers for news from the sources. It updates in 15 minute intervals and aggregates most developer accounts. In case you think I am missing one, shoot me a line and I will add him or her.

Who is behind this?

Armin Ronacher. You can contact me via mail or Twitter.

Why does this website exist?

Following the twitter accounts of all developers is not the problem, but also finding what they write into the forums is too much work. However every once in a while there is some interesting information in the forums which is why I decided to automatically collect that information.

There is too much information

Indeed. If you have some ideas how I could filter out useless tweets and stuff, let me know. I am already filtering out replies to other people from the tweets which reduced the amount of information here a lot, but it's still too much. I personally mostly use it to track the forum posts which are otherwise quite hard to find.

I am missing on this list / X is missing on this list

This website aggregates developers working on BF3 only. If you are missing or someone you know is missing, please let me know and provide this information: real name, twitter account, forums account, optionally a short description. If that developer does not have a twitter or forum account, one is enough obviously.

Where is the Code?

Good that you're asking. If you want to have a look at it, you can find the code on github: mitsuhiko/bf3-aggregator.

Can I get that data?

Send an HTTP request with Accept: application/json to any of the URLs returning tweets or forum posts and you get JSON back. That you can parse and use for your own purposes. This website updates every ~30 minutes, so don't bother requesting more. I don't have unlimited traffic on this server so please be kind and cache on your side.

Why sign in with Steam?

If you are logged in you can favorite items. This has the advantage that if enough users do that I can filter out more interesting items. You will also be able to find again items you found interesting in the past.

I would have linked to use the EA account system for that, but unfortunately they don't provide a service like steam which allows me to securely use the authentication system here. If you sign in with steam on this website no account information is transmitted to this website besides a unique number that keeps you apart from others and your current username (as well as real name if you have specified it).

If you don't have a steam account yet, you can create one for free on the steam website and use that to log into this website.
